What is the latest connectome for the Fly?

VFB

Short answer

  • The current leading full-brain connectomes for Drosophila are FAFB-based EM reconstructions:
  • Engert et al. 2022 EM FAFB dataset (A complete electron microscopy volume of the brain of Drosophila melanogaster). This is a major FAFB connectome resource; see the eLife publication and FlyBase/PubMed entries for details:
  • DOI: 10.7554/eLife.78110
  • PubMed: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35611959
  • FlyBase report: flybase.org/reports/FBrf0253662
  • The most recent FAFB-related dataset is the 2024 EM FAFB update (Gorko et al. 2024), i.e., EM FAFB Gorko et al. 2024.
  • This is listed in the Virtual Fly Brain data as EM FAFB Gorko et al. 2024 (Gorko2024).
  • The widely used hemibrain connectome (JRC_FlyEM_Hemibrain) is the other major modern fly connectome resource:
  • JRC_FlyEM_Hemibrain (hemibrain connectome) – latest public version shown in VFB as Neuprint interface entries “hemibrain:v1.2.1” and “hemibrain:v1.0.1”.
  • In VFB terms: JRC_FlyEM_Hemibrain (VFB_00101384) with painted-domain context (Xu2020 painted domains dataset Xu2020roi, Xu2020NeuronsV1point2point1).
  • This Hemibrain resource is the one people use for neuron-level connectivity in a large portion of the brain, accessible via Neuprint and integrated into VFB’s data model.

What this means for “latest”:

  • If you want the most comprehensive, full-brain connectome to date, the FAFB-based datasets are the foundation (Engert 2022 is the major published FAFB connectome; Gorko 2024 is the latest FAFB entry).
  • If you want the most up-to-date, neuron-to-neuron connectivity data for many adult brain regions with rapid query/search tooling, the JRC_FlyEM_Hemibrain (hemibrain) resource is the go-to, with v1.2.1 as the current front-runner version.
